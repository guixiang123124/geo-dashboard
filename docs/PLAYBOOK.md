## üî¥ Problem: Low Citation (Authority)
**Symptom**: AI mentions you but links to Amazon/Target or a blog instead of your site.

### üõ†Ô∏è Optimization Actions
1.  **Factual Density**: Add an "About Us" or "Technology/Materials" page rich in static facts (Founded date, Material composition, Safety certs). AI cites facts, not fluff.
2.  **URL Structure**: Use clear, semantic URLs (`/kids-safety-standards` vs `/page-123`).
3.  **Static HTML**: Ensure key informational content is server-side rendered, not buried in complex JavaScript.

## üî¥ Problem: Poor Representation (Framing)
**Symptom**: AI calls you "Cheap" when you are "Affordable Luxury", or misses your "Sustainable" angle.

### üõ†Ô∏è Optimization Actions
1.  **The "One-Liner"**: Place a clear, definitive definition of your brand in the Footer or About section. "Brand X is the leading provider of affordable organic cotton wear."
2.  **Consistency**: Audit product pages. If 50% say "Cheap" and 50% say "Value", AI gets confused. Unify terminology.
3.  **Disambiguation**: If you share a name with another entity, explicitly state "Not to be confused with..." or use schema markup.

## üî¥ Problem: Low Intent Coverage
**Symptom**: You appear for "Kids Clothes" but vanish for "Back to School Outfits".

### üõ†Ô∏è Optimization Actions
1.  **Contextual Hubs**: Create pages specifically for these intents (e.g., a "Back to School Guide").
2.  **FAQ Sections**: Add FAQs addressing specific needs ("Do you have sizes for toddlers?", "Is this durable for playground?").
3.  **Long-tail Keywords**: Ensure product descriptions include intent-based keywords ("Perfect for holiday parties", "Durable for active play").
